India Takes Commanding 1-0 Lead in ODI Series Against South Africa

In a commanding display, India took a formidable 1-0 lead in the ODI series against South Africa at the Wanderers. Opting to bat first, South Africa faced a relentless Indian pace attack led by Arshdeep Singh, who claimed a remarkable five-wicket haul. Avesh Khan provided crucial support, dismissing the hosts for just 116 runs within 28 overs.

The Proteas faced early setbacks as Reeza Hendricks departed for a duck, dragging Arshdeep onto his stumps. Rassie van der Dussen followed suit, falling for a golden duck. Despite Tony de Zorzi's counterattack, Arshdeep continued to wreak havoc, claiming his fourth wicket by dismissing Heinrich Klaasen. Avesh Khan joined the party, taking two quick wickets, reducing South Africa to 52/6.

David Miller's tame shot and Andile Phehlukwayo's fighting 33 were not enough to revive the innings. Arshdeep Singh completed his five-wicket haul as South Africa crumbled for 116.

In response, India chased the modest target with ease, losing Ruturaj Gaikwad early but showcasing a dominant performance. Debutant Sai Sudharsan impressed with a composed half-century, becoming the 17th Indian to achieve the feat on debut. Shreyas Iyer, too, made a brisk start, smashing boundaries as India comfortably reached 117/2 in 16.4 overs.

Key Moments:

  • Arshdeep Singh's five-wicket haul dismantled South Africa, leaving them reeling at 116.
  • Sai Sudharsan's impressive debut half-century and Shreyas Iyer's quick start ensured India's comfortable chase.
  • India's pace duo of Arshdeep Singh and Avesh Khan proved instrumental in restricting South Africa to a low total.

Brief Scores:

South Africa: 116 in 27.3 overs (Andile Phehlukwayo 33; Arshdeep Singh 5/37, Avesh Khan 4/27)

India: 117/2 in 16.4 overs (Sai Sudharsan 55*, Shreyas Iyer 52)

Result:

India won by 8 wickets
